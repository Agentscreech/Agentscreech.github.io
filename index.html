After over a decade of sales and business managment, I decided to follow a passion I've always wanted to do: Software Development

Background

My story begins in the late '80s where my step-father managed to have a 486DX computer. You know...the one that only ran MS-DOS and had a monochrome output?

He was able to teach me the basics of how to use it. He also showed me how to find out what commands were available to me and how to find out how to use them.

With that knowledge I found out that I could make texted based menus, draw rudementiary boxes with ASCII, and launch different programs by entering a single number...at 8 years old.

Growing up, I was the one that friends and family called to fixed their various electronics. Car stereos, home theaters, computers, home networks. I have always been able to learn and understand the concepts behind any thing technical extremely quickly and usually on my own.

This was a true calling for me at an early age. Later, in high school I was able to learn on my own, BASIC and wrote a program that took up the whopping 128kb memory of a TI-85 calulator. The program was a slot machine.

After High School I continued learning computer networking, hardware and operating system repair which got me a job as a Geek Squad agent at Best Buy

From that job I progressively learn business and sales management. That eventually lead to me starting my own business where I sold industrial grade scientific instrumentation.

My other hobbies include, movies, video games, car racing (autocross, trackdays, etc), beach volleyball and tennis.
